# :test_tube: The 4:6 Method Guide

> **"First 40% determines Balance (Sweet vs Acidic). Last 60% determines Strength."** â€” Tetsu Kasuya

The method divides total water into two phases:

## 1. Phase 1: Balance (First 40%)

The first two pours determine the flavor profile.

*   **Sweeter:** Pour smaller amount first (e.g. 50g $\rightarrow$ 70g).
*   **Acidic:** Pour larger amount first (e.g. 70g $\rightarrow$ 50g).
*   **Balanced:** Pour equal amounts (e.g. 60g $\rightarrow$ 60g).

## 2. Phase 2: Strength (Last 60%)

The remaining water determines the strength/concentration.

*   **Light Intensity:** 1 Pour.
*   **Medium Intensity:** 2 Pours.
*   **Strong Intensity:** 3 Pours.

## 3. The Rules

1.  **Grind:** Coarse (Coarser than V60 standard).
2.  **Timing:** Wait 45 seconds between *every* pour.
3.  **Drawdown:** The water should drain completely before the next pour.

!!! tip "Example Recipe (20g Coffee / 300g Water)"
    *   **Phase 1 (120g total):**
        *   Pour 1: 50g (0:00 - 0:45)
        *   Pour 2: 70g (0:45 - 1:30)
    *   **Phase 2 (180g total):**
        *   Pour 3: 60g (1:30 - 2:15)
        *   Pour 4: 60g (2:15 - 3:00)
        *   Pour 5: 60g (3:00 - 3:45)

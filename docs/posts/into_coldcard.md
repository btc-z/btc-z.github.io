# From Coinbase to ColdCard

The ownership of Bitcoin depends on only the owners having the private key. Any third parties, including Coinbase, having access to the owners private key automatically implies incomplete ownership, which can later on be compromised. This is commonly known as the motto "not your keys, not your coins".

Not only this, any third instituions are subject to legal risks. For example, two days ago, on march 27, 2024, Coinbase loses most of motion to dismiss SEC lawsuit [link](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2024/03/27/coinbase-loses-most-of-motion-to-dismiss-sec-lawsuit/). Last year summer, SEC charged Coinbase for operating as an unregistered Securities Exchange, broker and clearing agency [link](https://www.sec.gov/news/press-release/2023-102). The thought provokng question is: "do you want to bear the legal risk that Coinbase is involved when instead you can custody your own Bitcoin?"

From self custodying my own bitcoin for more than 3 years now, I think the largest challenge in self custody is lack of educational materials of how to do it. Therefore, this article dedicates to promoting the knowledge of how to securely transfer your Bitcoin off Coinbase to your personal hardware wallet. I have written a separate article explaning how to set up self-custody from scratch [here](./self_custody.md) or you can just ask me via DMs.

Keep in mind, self custody is a choice, and it is not a risk-free choice. A risk free choie simply doesn't exist in this world. Do you trust yourself not losing the key or do you trust custodian never deny your access to your money? Remember history.
